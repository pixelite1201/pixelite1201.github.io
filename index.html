About Me

I am currently a Research Engineer at the Max Planck Institute for Intelligent Systems, with a focus on developing robust and scalable pipelines for 3D human modeling. My research is anchored in synthetic data generation, 3D registration, and monocular pose estimation. I hold a Master's degree in Computer Science from IIT Bombay, where I developed tools for 2D animation interfaces.

Prior experience includes roles as Data Scientist at Zapr Media Labs and Lead Engineer at Samsung R&D, Bangalore. I bring a strong software engineering background and a passion for computational geometry and visual understanding.

Projects and Publications

BEDLAM: Lifelike Animated Motion Dataset (CVPR 2023)

Michael J. Black, Priyanka Patel, Joachim Tesch, Jinlong Yang

BEDLAM features realistic RGB videos with simulated 3D humans in varied clothing and environments. My contributions include regressor training, ablations, and benchmark evaluations.

Project Page

AGORA: Avatars in Geography Optimized for Regression Analysis (CVPR 2021)

Priyanka Patel, Chun-Hao P. Huang, Joachim Tesch, David T. Hoffmann, Shashank Tripathi, Michael J. Black

AGORA is a synthetic dataset addressing domain gaps in 3D human pose and shape estimation. It includes SMPL/SMPL-X data with diverse clothing and lighting for model development and evaluation.

Project Page

COMA in PyTorch

COMA (Convolutional Mesh Autoencoder) offers superior representation of 3D facial shapes. I ported the original TensorFlow code to PyTorch using torch-geometric.

Code

3D Model Registration Pipeline

Designed a modular registration pipeline for SMPL/SMPL-X models to 3D scans. Features include multi-view rendering, keypoint projection loss, and surface-based optimization.

Registration for Clothed 3D Scans

Enhanced SMPL-X registration for clothed scans using Graphonomy to segment skin and clothing, optimizing geometry alignment while preserving anatomical realism.
